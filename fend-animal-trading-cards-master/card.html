Malagasy Giant Chameleon

Chameleons have a third eye in the center of their foreheads. It is a rudimentary eye that is essentially a spot of pigment connected to the pineal gland by a nerve. It helps in determining light from darkness, and is able to detect varying levels of ultraviolet light.

  • Scientific Name: Furcifer Oustaleti
  • Average Length: Maximum total length (including tail) of 68.5 cm (27 in
  • Average Life Span: Up to 10 years in the wild
  • Habitat: Degraded habitats, agricultural land and human settlements, undisturbed dry forest, savannah
